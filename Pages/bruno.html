Background:

Dr. Bruno Andrade is a Brazilian Researcher based in Cork, Ireland, at the Department of Computer Sciences of Munster Technological University. His main research line involves the relationship between a given microbiome and its host/environment to search for biomarkers that could explain differences in the immunological response to infections in humans and performance phenotypes in livestock, thus, having a potential biotechnological impact.

Originally a Biotechnologist, he has a Master’s and Ph.D. degrees in Computational Biology and was a post-doctoral fellow twice. The first time as a FAPESP fellow at EMBRAPA/Brazil (GRANT_NUMBER: 2019/04089-2), and the second time an MSCA fellow at MTU and Adaptcentre (GRANT_NUMBER: 801522), part of the ELITE-S: Future Leadership in ICT Standards in Europe.

Currently, he is a Lecturer of Computer sciences at MTU, teaching Computational Biology and scientific programming. Research-wise, he is part of exciting projects involving Artificial Intelligence and Microbiome, working to develop new methodologies for Microbiome Data Analysis and to discover new biomarkers/probiotics.
